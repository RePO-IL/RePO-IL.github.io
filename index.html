Reactive Policies with Observation Augmentation

Abstract

Robotic imitation learning (IL) in dynamic environments, where object positions or external forces change unpredictably, poses a major challenge for current state-of-the-art methods. These methods often rely on multi-step, open-loop action execution for temporal consistency, but this approach hinders reactivity and adaptation under dynamic conditions. As a response, we propose RePO, a robust and responsive IL framework that predicts single-step actions based on a sequence of past image observations. To mitigate the susceptibility to covariate shift that arises from longer observation horizons, RePO injects adversarial noise into low-dimensional spatial image embeddings during training. This enhances robustness to out-of-distribution states while preserving semantic content. The framework can be used with any transformer-based action policy. We present two variants, RePO-BCT and RePO-DP, and show that using RePO consistently improves model performance on a diverse set of real and simulated robotic tasks featuring unpredictable perturbations. Our results demonstrate that the proposed method enables both temporally consistent and reactive policy learning for dynamic robotic tasks.

Our proposed approach, RePO, uses single-step action prediction coupled with a longer observation context, enabling reactive action execution while preserving temporal action consistency. To mitigate the increased risk of covariate shift that comes with extending the context, RePO applies a carefully designed noise augmentation scheme during training and inference. Crucially, noise perturbation is applied to the low-dimensional, spatial image representations produced by the (Resnet+SpatialSoftmax) vision backbone, encouraging robustness while preserving image semantics and intrinsic structure. We combine RePO with two different action policies, a Behavior Cloning Transformer (RePO-BCT), and a Diffusion Policy Transformer (RePO-DP).

RePO is evaluated on a set of challenging, dynamic robot tasks. For the simulation tasks, we compare RePO-BCT and RePO-DP with the vanilla BC transformer without noise augmentation, and with multiple variants of Diffusion Policy with different action horizons and observation history lengths.

Simulation tasks

RePO outperforms the baselines across all simulation tasks, exhibiting smooth and reactive control.

Real-world experiment

In the dynamic real-world experiment, the agents must place a hanging object in a narrow cup, while compensating for unpredictable actuator drift. RePO-BCT shows improved resilience, and significantly outperforms vanilla BCT, Consistency Policy and Diffusion Policy.
